About Me

Hello, I am Matthew Lee, a second year at UC Berkeley studying Elecrical Engineering and Computer Sciences. I am passionate about all things hardware and software, with a current emphasis in learning more about analog design and computer architecture.

I am currently taking classes on Microelectronic Devices and Circuits, learning the fundamental circuit and semiconductor device concepts needed to understand analog integrated circuits; Signals and Systems, learning the background behind CT and DT analysis alongside communication and feedback systems; Computer Architecture, learning the fundamentals of the entire computer architecture stack

At Berkeley I am also the Electrical Subteam lead for CalSol where I apply PCB Design, PCB Verification, and Electrical System Integration in a large team setting. I am also a director for the CALICO Informatics Competition where I hone my leadership and programming skills by pushing out all the problems of a semesterly competitive programming competition. I also am a member of the IEEE branch in Berkeley, as well as a member of the service committee in HKN. I also am an electronics admin for the student EE makerspace called Supernode, where I lead safety trainings and maintain the spaces effectiveness.

Outside of school, I design custom guitar pedals and play electric guitar. I am interested in amateur astronomy, discussing philosophy, and listening to alternative rock.
